Thyme Traveler

star_rate star_rate star_rate star_rate_half star_rate
7 Wishing Well Way, Hidden Valley, GP 91820

Embark on a culinary adventure through time at Thyme Traveler, where every dish is a portal to a different era! Savor the rich spices of ancient Rome in our aromatic lamb tagine, or dance to the vibrant flavors of 1920s New Orleans with our jazz-era gumbo. Feeling wanderlust? Take a bite of the Silk Road with our stir-fried Mongolian chicken, or set sail for the high seas with our hearty Viking stew. Each plate is a meticulously crafted masterpiece, transporting you to the heart of history with every bite. So grab your fork and passport, and let Thyme Traveler whisk you away on a delicious journey through time!

By Jane Doe on 02/22/2024
Thyme Traveler isn't just a restaurant; it's a time machine for your taste buds. Each bite whisks you to a different era, from the smoky, umami-rich Roman flatbread starter to the melt-in-your-mouth saffron chicken tagine that transports you to a sun-drenched Moroccan courtyard. The creativity here is boundless, with dishes like "Medieval Meatloaf with Fig Glaze" and "Renaissance Ravioli with Pistachio Pesto" offering playful twists on historical cuisines. But the innovation never overshadows the quality of the ingredients. Every dish is a celebration of fresh, seasonal produce, cooked to perfection. Even the desserts are masterpieces, like the "Victorian Rosewater Panna Cotta" or the "Egyptian Spiced Baklava," each a sweet finale to your time-traveling adventure. Service is knowledgeable and passionate about the culinary history woven into each dish, enhancing the immersive experience. Thyme Traveler is more than just a meal; it's an unforgettable journey through taste and time. 5/5 stars, a must-try for anyone with a sense of adventure.

By John Doe on 12/24/2023
Thyme Traveler offers a comfortable stop on your culinary journey, a place where familiar flavors get a sophisticated twist. The atmosphere is warm and inviting, with exposed brick walls and a soundtrack of classic pop hits. The menu features traditional dishes reimagined with fresh, local ingredients. Start with the "Grandma's Mac and Cheese with Truffle Oil," a creamy embrace with a touch of luxury. The "Thyme-Crusted Salmon with Lemon Beurre Blanc" is a classic comfort food elevated by vibrant herb notes. Portions are generous, and prices are reasonable, making it a great spot for a casual yet satisfying meal. Service is friendly and efficient, keeping the focus on enjoying the food and catching up with friends. While Thyme Traveler might not take you to foreign lands, it does a fantastic job of elevating everyday dishes, making it a reliable and enjoyable choice for any occasion. 4/5 stars, perfect for a cozy and delicious night out.

By Chris Seed on 12/20/2023
Thyme Traveler's lofty menu promises an exciting voyage through culinary history, but sadly, the execution leaves much to be desired. The ambition is evident in the quirky dish names and creative descriptions, but it often feels like style over substance. The "Roman Flatbread with Roasted Figs and Gorgonzola" arrived soggy and undercooked, the figs more mush than sweetness. The "Renaissance Ravioli with Pistachio Pesto" tasted more like bland pasta shells than an innovative fusion. Even the dessert, the "Egyptian Spiced Baklava," disappointed with its overly syrupy filling and lack of distinct spice. Service was well-meaning but overwhelmed, with long wait times and forgotten orders adding to the frustration. Thyme Traveler has the potential to be something special, but it needs to focus on quality over quantity, refining its dishes and ensuring consistency. 2/5 stars, a promising concept in need of some serious fine-tuning.
